Web 1.0

Web 1.0 refers to the first era of the World Wide Web, roughly from 1991 to 2004. It was characterized by static HTML websites, one-way communication, and very limited user interaction. Users could only read content — they had no ability to comment, post, or interact with pages the way we do today.

Web 1.0 sites were like digital brochures — companies and individuals published information and visitors consumed it passively. There was no social media, no user-generated content, and pages rarely updated. Despite its limitations, Web 1.0 was revolutionary for its time as it made information globally accessible for the very first time.

💡 Did you know? Early Web 1.0 pages often used animated GIFs, bright backgrounds, and visitor counters — design choices that feel very retro today but were cutting-edge in the 1990s!
