In the quiet corridors of the **Svalbard Global Seed Vault**, located deep inside a sandstone mountain on a remote Norwegian island, lies the world’s ultimate "reset button" for agriculture. Often called the **Doomsday Vault**, it houses over a million unique seed samples from nearly every country on Earth, kept at a constant $-18$°C to ensure their viability for centuries. This isn't just a museum for plants; it’s a fail-safe against local gene bank failures, war, and climate change. Interestingly, the vault saw its first major withdrawal in 2015 due to the Syrian Civil War, proving that this icy fortress isn't just a hypothetical precaution—it's a living, breathing insurance policy for human civilization.
