Extranet

An Extranet is a controlled, private network that allows authorized external partners — such as suppliers, clients, or business partners — to access specific parts of a company's intranet. It sits between the intranet and the public internet, acting as a middle layer that extends limited internal access to trusted outsiders.

For example, a company might give a supplier access to their inventory system through an extranet, without exposing the rest of their internal network. As shown in the network diagram, the intranet (LAN) is the innermost layer, the extranet surrounds it, and the internet is the outermost public layer.

💡 Did you know? Many airline booking systems that travel agents use are actually extranets — giving agents access to airline inventory without making it fully public!
