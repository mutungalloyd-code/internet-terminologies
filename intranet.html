Intranet

An Intranet is a private, internal network used exclusively within an organization. It uses the same technologies as the internet — such as browsers, web pages, and HTTP — but access is restricted to authorized employees only. It is the innermost layer of a company's network, sitting inside the extranet and internet layers.

Organizations use intranets to share internal documents, company news, HR resources, and communication tools securely. Think of it as a company's own private mini-internet that only staff can access, often requiring a login and sometimes a VPN when working remotely.

💡 Did you know? The term "intranet" first appeared in the mid-1990s. Today, popular intranet platforms include Microsoft SharePoint and Google Sites.
